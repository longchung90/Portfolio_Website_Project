Summary:

  • The form currently does not actually send the message or the sender’s details anywhere.
  • You do not receive notifications or details by email or any other means.
  • Submissions stay only on the visitor’s browser and do not reach you.

How to Receive Notifications and Details

To actually receive messages and contact details, you need to connect your form to a backend service or a third-party form handler. Here are two common approaches:

1. Use a Third-Party Form Service (No backend coding required)

  • Services: Formspree, Formsubmit, Getform, etc.
  • How: You change your form’s <form> tag to point to the service, and they email you the details.

Example with Formspree:

  • You sign up at Formspree and get your form ID.
  • You receive each submission as an email.

2. Build a Backend (Advanced)

  • You host a server (Node.js, Python, PHP, etc.) that receives the form data using a POST request.
  • The backend then sends you an email or stores messages in a database.

This option is more complex and requires server hosting. For most personal portfolios, option 1 is easiest.

What to Change in Your Code

  • Update your <form> tag with the action/method and add name attributes to the inputs.
  • Remove the JavaScript that just shows the alert (or keep it for confirmation after the form is submitted).

If you want step-by-step help to set up one of these options, let me know which method you prefer (third-party service or your own backend), and I can walk you through the exact changes!
